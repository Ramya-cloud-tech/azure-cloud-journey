Day 1 – Foundations of Cloud Computing

📅 Date: 19 June 2026

🌐 What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services such as servers, storage, databases, networking, and software over the internet.

Instead of buying and maintaining physical hardware, organizations can access these resources on demand and pay only for what they use.

💰 CapEx vs OpEx

One of the biggest advantages of cloud computing is the shift from CapEx (Capital Expenditure) to OpEx (Operational Expenditure).

CapEx (Traditional IT)

Buy physical servers and equipment
Large upfront investment
Company manages and maintains its own infrastructure

OpEx (Cloud Computing)

Pay-as-you-go pricing
No large upfront investment
Cloud provider manages the infrastructure

Key takeaway: Cloud computing allows organizations to reduce costs and become more flexible.

☁️ Cloud Deployment Models

🌍 Public Cloud

Infrastructure is owned and managed by a cloud provider such as Microsoft Azure and shared across multiple customers.

🏢 Private Cloud

Infrastructure is dedicated to a single organization, providing greater control and security.

🔗 Hybrid Cloud

Combines on-premises infrastructure with public cloud services, allowing organizations to gradually adopt cloud technologies.

⭐ Key Benefits of Cloud Computing
📈 Scalability – Increase or decrease resources based on demand.
⚡ Agility – Deploy resources in minutes instead of weeks.
💵 Cost Efficiency – Pay only for what is used.
🛡️ High Availability – Keep services online with minimal downtime.
🔄 Reliability – Recover quickly from failures and continue operating.
🧠 Reflection

Today I learned that cloud computing is more than simply storing files online.

Cloud technology helps organizations build scalable, reliable, and highly available systems without owning physical infrastructure.

I also learned how businesses can reduce costs, improve efficiency, and deploy services much faster than traditional IT environments.
